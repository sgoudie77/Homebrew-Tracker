JSB Brewing Method

1. Clean and sanitize all equipment: fermenter, lid, and mixing spoon with sanitizing detergent.
2. Place the beer can or pouch in hot water for 15 minutes to soften the extract.
3. Boil 4 litres of water in a large pot, add the contents of the tin or pouch and corn sugar.
4. Rinse out any remains from the tin or pouch with hot water, and mix everything well.
5. Pour mixture into a sanitized fermenter, and top up to 23 litres with cold tap water or other clean water.
6. Cover the fermenter and let stand until temperature is below 25°C, and then sprinkle in the yeast.
7. Fill the airlock halfway with water, and put in the fermentor lid.
8. Wait for up to 10 days until the airlock has stopped bubbling, keeping the fermenter around 20-25°C.
9. Transfer to a carboy midway to reduce sediment.
10. Check the alcohol level now using a sterilized hydrometer. The value should be equal or below the listed value.
11. Clean and sanitize bottles, siphoning hose and bottler, and siphon back into a fermenter bucket again.
12. Lift your fermenter onto a high surface before bottling to allow gravity to help settle sediment that may have moved.
13. Dissolve your priming sugar in 500ml of boiling water, let cool, and mix well into the fermenter.
14. Siphon from the fermenter into the bottles, leaving 5cm airspace at the top when filling each bottle.
15. Store your bottles in a dark place at room temperature for 2 weeks to reduce cloudiness during carbonization.
